About Me

MY STORY.

"The habits I made in the gym, changed every aspect within my life." — Kyler Brooker

My Story.

I was 18 years old with less than $1,000 to my name. My family wasn't around, nobody to call. I won't get into everything that happened, but I was in a really dark place. Multiple times I didn't think I was going to make it.

I decided I wasn't going to let that be it.

My alarm went off at 4:30 in the morning, in the gym by 5, then straight to work at 6, and I wouldn't clock out until 10 at night. I did this on repeat. I was still in school on top of all of that, trying to hold everything together. Nobody was telling me I was doing a good job or even knew what I was going through. I just kept going.

I didn't even like it at first, but I knew if I stopped, nothing would change. I was done with nothing changing.

I've Built a Life I Didn't Think I Deserved.

I genuinely mean this. There were nights where I did not want to be here anymore. I'd find myself at a park at 12am, staring up into the sky thinking the world would be just fine without me in it. I believed that for a while. I thought I was too far behind while others were getting their lives in order. I thought I was too much of a mess for things to ever get better for me.

I was wrong.

I'm almost 20 years old now. I have a body I actually put real work into. I've built something from literally nothing. I'm nowhere close to where I want to be yet. But I'm so far from where I once was. I know that whatever gets thrown at me in the future, I've already gotten through something much worse.

If You Can Do It In There,
You Can Do It Out There.

My friend told me this one day in the gym. When he said it, I just stood there. Because he was right. Everything I figured out about myself was underneath a bar. That part of me that doesn't quit, that keeps showing up even when I don't have a reason to, I carry that with me everywhere I go. It's why I work the way I work. It's how I handle things when they get hard outside the gym. It's why so many people perceive me as the hardest worker in the room.

I used to have this voice in my head telling me I wasn't worth anything in this life. That I'd never become something. I heard it constantly for years. I know this voice very well. And what I'll tell you is that it was lying to me.

It's lying to you too.

Where I'm At Now.

I dropped out of UW.

Every day I would sit in class and realize I just don't want to learn this. The material never sparked any interest in me. Can you imagine that? A 4.0 high school student dropping out of some people's dream college. Every lecture I'd think to myself that I could be building something else instead. Something I'm actually interested in. So I left. I walked away from what I was supposed to do and went after what I actually want. I'm coaching online now, competing in powerlifting meets, and doing things I never thought I was going to be able to do.

I have younger siblings watching me. That means more to me than I can really put into words. I want them to look at me one day and think — okay, if he did it, I can do something great too. I want to be something real that they can actually point to and look up to. Not just talk, but living, walking proof that their dreams are accomplishable.

This applies to everyone I coach. Everyone I encounter in life. I want people to walk away thinking to themselves that they're much more capable than they give themselves credit for. I know what it's like to believe you're not. I know exactly how wrong that feeling is because I've lived it.

You get one life. I started living when I realized that. I've wasted enough of mine thinking I wasn't worth fighting for. I'm done with that. If you're reading this, I hope you are too.

Why I Want To Help Others.

I've never had someone in my corner. Nobody showed me what was right and wrong. Nobody held me accountable. Nobody actually gave a fuck whether I made it or not. I had to figure everything out alone and it cost me time I'll never get back.

That's exactly why I coach. I don't want you to waste years guessing. I don't want you to go through the same process as me and guess what works. I've already done that. So you don't have to.

When you work with me, you get everything I wish I had. A real plan, someone who's been through it, and a person who genuinely wants to see you win.

I'm not a licensed therapist or a doctor. I'm a 19-year-old who lived it. The rock bottom, the rebuilding, all of it. You can't learn that in a classroom. You can only earn it.

My Mission

Most people aren't failing because they're lazy. They're failing because nobody ever gave them the right structure. My job is to be the person who finally does that for you and stays with you every step of the way until you get there.
